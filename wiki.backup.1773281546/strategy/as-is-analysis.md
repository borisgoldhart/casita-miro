# As-Is Analysis

**Website Audit, Market Context, Competitive Positioning**

---

## Current State Summary

### Casita Miro Today
- **Location:** Waiheke Island, New Zealand (on-premise and online)
- **Operating since:** 1994 (vineyard); restaurant for 25+ years
- **Reputation:** 4.7/5 on TripAdvisor (#1 in Onetangi), 1,758 reviews, 13-year award streak
- **Awards:** TripAdvisor Traveller's Choice, Iconic Eats Top 100, Lewisham Award, Cuisine Magazine Good Food
- **Business Model:** Restaurant + vineyard + cellar door + wine retail + food products + events

### Website Audit
- **Pages:** ~25-30 pages exist (not just a landing page)
- **Content Quality:** Skeletal — pages exist but lack depth, detail, imagery, pricing
- **E-Commerce:** Functional shop exists but products not well-promoted
- **Bookings:** Online system functional; intimidating T&Cs ($30 no-show, 2-hour limit)
- **Key Gaps:** No menus, no wine lists, no pricing, no food photography, no storytelling

### Current Revenue Streams
✅ On-site dining (Fiesta menu, casual cellar door)  
✅ Wine retail (on-site + e-cellardoor international shipping)  
✅ Events (weddings, groups, private dining)  
✅ Food products (olive oil, olives, honey, walnuts, pesto — sold to local vendors)  
✅ Proprietary products (Madame Rouge aperitif)  

❌ NOT OPTIMIZED: Wine club, e-commerce, national distribution, winter revenue

---

## Market Opportunity

### Waiheke Island Tourism
- **1.3 million unique visitors annually** (2016-17 data)
- **Summer population:** 9,000 → 30,000+ (peak season)
- **World-class destination:** Conde Nast Top 20 Islands; Lonely Planet Top 10
- **Wine tourism focus:** 30+ wineries, premium wine tours, experiential packages
- **Competitive context:** Many wine bars, restaurants; Casita Miro is ONLY Spanish+vineyard combo

### Spanish Dining in NZ
- **Auckland has Spanish restaurants** (Madriz, Alma, Carmen Jones)
- **BUT none with own vineyard or Waiheke location advantage**
- **Casita Miro's unique positioning:** Only Spanish restaurant + vineyard on island; defensible niche

### Boutique Wine Experience Trends
- **Premium positioning model** (Craggy Range, The Landing, Latitude 45)
- **Multi-revenue streams:** Restaurant + wine sales + experiences + accommodation
- **Narrative-driven:** Founder story, terroir, craftsmanship
- **Pricing:** $100-400+ per person for immersive experiences
- **Casita Miro opportunity:** Develop premium wine education and multi-day packages

## Digital Presence Assessment

### Strengths
✅ Domain owned  
✅ Multiple pages (25-30)  
✅ Online booking system  
✅ E-commerce shop functional  
✅ TripAdvisor link (leveraging #1 rating)  
✅ Social media links (Facebook, Instagram)  
✅ Email signup (Casita Amigo)  

### Weaknesses
❌ Content extremely thin across pages  
❌ No menus, wine lists, or pricing visible  
❌ No food or lifestyle photography  
❌ No storytelling or brand narrative  
❌ No content marketing (blog appears unused)  
❌ Poor SEO optimization  
❌ No active social media strategy visible  
❌ Email signup has no follow-up (unused list)  

### Content Detail Gaps
- **Dining:** Menu items, descriptions, and prices **NOT shown**
- **Wine:** Varietals listed but no vintages, tasting notes, or pricing
- **Products:** Exist but not highlighted or explained
- **Vineyard:** Family story present but not woven throughout
- **About:** No dedicated page explaining restaurant history or team

## SWOT Snapshot

### Strengths
- 13-year award streak; 4.7/5 rating (1,758 reviews)
- Only Spanish+vineyard combo on Waiheke
- Own 4.2-hectare vineyard (margin control)
- Multiple revenue streams (diversified)
- Experienced leadership (25+ years)
- Premium location (closest to Onetangi Beach)
- Proprietary products (Madame Rouge, oils, walnuts, honey)

### Opportunities
- Website and digital transformation
- Wine club and education programs
- Food product retail expansion
- National and international distribution
- Influencer and media partnerships
- E-commerce and direct-to-consumer
- Event programming (winter focus)
- Product line extensions (merch, cookbooks)

### Threats
- Increased tourism = more competition
- Economic downturn (discretionary spending risk)
- Travel disruptions (ferry access critical)
- Supply chain risks (Spanish imports)
- Small team limits scaling

---

## Strategic Implications

**Reputation is their moat:** 13-year award streak + #1 rating = defensible competitive advantage; need to amplify through digital storytelling.

**Digital presence is biggest gap:** Website/SEO/social severely underdeveloped relative to competitors and award status; quick wins possible here.

**Pricing opacity = friction:** No visible pricing = customers don't book/buy; need transparent pricing + packages.

**Multiple streams underdeveloped:** Wine club, events, products not fully realized; structured programs could 2-3x revenue.
