# Casita Miro - SWOT Analysis (Farm-to-Table & Spanish Provenance)
**Date:** March 12, 2026  
**Focus:** Farm-to-table excellence, Spanish sourcing heritage, product expansion, premium positioning

---

## STRENGTHS

### Reputation & Market Position
✅ **13-year award streak** (TripAdvisor 2011-2024) — #1 restaurant in Onetangi, 4.7/5 (1,758 reviews)  
✅ **Only Spanish restaurant + own vineyard** on Waiheke — defensible niche with integrated wine story  
✅ **Premium island destination** — Conde Nast Top 20 Islands; summer population 30,000+ visitors  
✅ **Authentic Spanish credentials** — Direct sourcing from Spanish bodegas, family 25+ years in Mediterranean cuisine  

### Farm-to-Table Advantage
✅ **Own 4.2-hectare vineyard** — estate wines, full supply chain control, terroir authenticity  
✅ **Organic kitchen gardens** — fresh herbs, salad leaves, citrus, vegetables, olives (showcased on every plate)  
✅ **On-premise food production** — growing proprietary products: olive oil, walnuts, honey, olives, pesto  
✅ **Integrated sourcing story** — Spain + Waiheke farms = unique "authentic European ingredient + local terroir" positioning  

### Product & Experience Range
✅ **Multiple revenue streams** — dining (Fiesta menu), cellar door (casual tastings), wine retail, events, food products, Madame Rouge aperitif  
✅ **Proprietary products with margins** — Madame Rouge, Miro Vineyard wines, branded olive oil, olives, honey, walnuts, pesto  
✅ **Experienced leadership** — Barnett & Cat Bond: 25+ years Mediterranean food/wine mastery; family commitment to quality  

---

## CONSIDERATIONS (Reframed Weaknesses)

**Limited dining footprint** → Opportunity for intimacy and premium positioning (not everyone wants 200-seat restaurants)  
**Fiesta menu only** → Opportunity to develop tiered tasting menus showcasing farm-to-table ingredients  
**Seasonal cellar door operations** → Opportunity to develop covered year-round tasting space  
**Single location** → Opportunity to deepen brand identity around one authentic, intimate place (vs. diluting across venues)  
**No accommodation** → Focus on exceptional day experience + events rather than lodging complexity

## THREATS

**Market & Competition**
- Increased Waiheke tourism could bring competing Spanish/Mediterranean restaurants  
- Economic downturn impacts discretionary fine dining and wine tourism spending  
- Travel disruptions (ferry strikes, fuel costs) affect island accessibility  

**Operational**
- Supply chain: reliance on Spanish imports could face trade/shipping disruptions  
- Climate: extreme weather impacts vineyard yields and garden productivity  
- Labor costs on island are higher than urban areas  
- Small team limits simultaneous operations  

**Market Trends**
- Health-conscious shift away from alcohol (though farm-to-table positioning helps)  
- Younger demographics may prefer casual over formal dining  

---

## STRATEGIC PRIORITIES

### 1. **Amplify Farm-to-Table as Core Brand**
- Market organic gardens and Spanish provenance as primary differentiator
- Make sourcing (Spain + Waiheke) visible in every menu, every story, every communication
- Position Casita Miro as "authentic Spanish ingredients on Waiheke soil" — unique in world

### 2. **Develop Tiered Tasting Experiences**
- Casual cellar door: $25-40 (walk-in tastings, casual tapas)
- Mid-tier tastings: $75-100 (sommelier-led, farm-to-table pairing)
- Premium chef's experiences: $150-200 (limited seats, garden focus, exclusive wines)
- Wine club: recurring revenue, educational content, quarterly releases

### 3. **Food Product Commercialization**
- Scale farm production: olive oil, olives, honey, walnuts, pesto as retail products
- Online direct-to-consumer shop for Casita Miro products
- Specialty food retail partnerships (Auckland, national)
- Gift sets and seasonal bundles

### 4. **Spanish Provenance Storytelling**
- Clear communication: where ingredients come from, why they matter, how they're sourced
- Highlight Spanish traditions in cooking and wine
- Create authority position: Casita Miro as Spanish food/wine expert in NZ

### 5. **Content-Driven Discovery**
- Blog, social, video content showing gardens, sourcing, seasonal changes
- Media outreach: food/wine publications, travel press, food/wine influencers
- Build organic traffic around farm-to-table and Spanish food/wine keywords
