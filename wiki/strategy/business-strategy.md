# Business Strategy Update

**Strategic Vision, Revenue Model, 12-Month Roadmap**

---

## Strategic Vision

**What Casita Miro Aspires to Be (3-5 years):**

Casita Miro is the **definitive Spanish food & wine destination in Australasia** — where authentic Spanish cuisine, Waiheke terroir, and premium Spanish goods converge.

**Like Movida (Melbourne), we're known for:**
- Spanish-first positioning (authentic, not fusion)
- Bold aesthetic and thoughtful product curation
- Seasonal ingredient storytelling
- Premium but approachable brand personality
- Merchandise and products as brand extension

---

## Business Model Strategy

### Three Interlocking Revenue Streams

**1. RESTAURANT (Year-round, winter-focused)**
- Fiesta menu (premium dining)
- Cellar door casual (accessible entry point)
- Events: weddings, corporate, private dining
- Winter strategy: Event programming, celebrations, covered year-round operation

**2. WINE (Build significantly)**
- Wine club (3 tiers: $75, $150, $250/quarter) — recurring revenue
- Wine shop (curated Miro + Spanish imports + sherries)
- E-commerce + international shipping
- Sommelier-led education and content
- Target: 25-30% of total revenue

**3. KITCHEN/FOOD PRODUCTS (New growth area)**
- Artisan products: olive oil, olives, honey, walnuts, pesto
- Specialty food retail partnerships (national)
- E-commerce direct-to-consumer
- Gift sets and subscription boxes
- Branded merchandise (ceramics, glassware, cookbooks)
- Target: 15-20% of total revenue

## Addressing Cat's Three Core Challenges

### Challenge #1: Winter Slow Season Revenue

**Current:** Closed/slow Nov-March (peak global tourism season)  
**Target:** Winter revenue = 60% of summer (vs. current 40-50%)

**Solutions:**
- **Year-round event programming** — wine releases, chef's dinners, corporate team-building, seasonal celebrations
- **Covered cellar door** — weather-independent walk-in operation year-round
- **International e-commerce** — winter = Northern Hemisphere holiday gift-giving peak
- **Corporate entertainment** — Auckland companies planning off-sites in winter

**Impact:** +20-25% annual revenue

---

### Challenge #2: Selling More Kitchen Products

**Current:** Products sold only to local vendors (<5% of revenue)  
**Target:** Product revenue = 15-20% of total

**Solutions:**
- **Redesign on-site shop** — destination experience with tastings and storytelling
- **Launch e-commerce** — products, Spanish imports, gift sets, subscriptions
- **National retail partnerships** — specialty food shops, gourmet retailers, online platforms
- **Restaurant upsell** — display products on tables, menu callouts, gift bundles

**Impact:** +10-20% revenue growth

---

### Challenge #3: Selling More Wine

**Current:** Wine retail ~15% of revenue (underdeveloped)  
**Target:** Wine revenue = 25-30% of total

**Solutions:**
- **Wine club (3 tiers)** — $75, $150, $250/quarter; recurring revenue
- **Curated wine shop** — Miro wines (core), Spanish regional, premium sherries
- **E-commerce** — full wine catalog, international shipping
- **Sommelier-led content** — blog, email, social education and marketing
- **International marketing** — Australia winter = holiday gift-giving season

**Impact:** +10-15% revenue growth

## Strategic Summary

**Core Strategy:** Build a premium Spanish brand (Movida model) with three interlocking revenue streams addressing winter seasonality, product retail, and wine growth.

**Competitive Advantage:** Only place combining authentic Spanish cuisine + own vineyard + artisan farm-to-table + premium Spanish goods + award-winning reputation.

**Organizational Priorities:**
1. Fix winter seasonality (events + e-commerce)
2. Develop wine club (recurring revenue)
3. Commercialize food products (retail + online)
4. Build Spanish-first brand narrative
5. Create content-driven discovery (organic growth)
