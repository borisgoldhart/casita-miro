## STRATEGIC VISION

**What Casita Miro Aspires to Be (3-5 years):**

**The "must go" culinary experience in New Zealand** — with an international reputation for the **most authentic Spanish food experience outside of Spain**. 

We're not just a restaurant; we're a destination. A place where guests from around the world come specifically for Casita Miro because nowhere else offers this level of Spanish authenticity combined with Waiheke terroir and Barnett & Cat's family story. Like Movida in Melbourne, we're known for **Spanish-first positioning**, **bold aesthetic**, **seasonal ingredient storytelling**, and **thoughtful product curation** — but we go deeper: we own our vineyard, grow our own ingredients, import directly from Spanish bodegas, and tell the story of why.

**Key Positioning:**
- Spanish heritage is absolutely core (not Mediterranean fusion)
- Waiheke vineyard and organic gardens amplify Spanish traditions (not distract from them)
- Premium but approachable (sophisticated, old world, comfortable, never stuffy)
- Every product, every meal, every experience tells the Spanish story
- International reputation for authenticity — the real deal, not imitation

## ADDRESSING CAT'S THREE CORE CHALLENGES

### Challenge #1: Winter Slow Season Revenue
**Problem:** Restaurant, wine tasting, store sales drop Nov-March (peak global tourism season)

**Solution Strategy:**

**A. Year-Round Event Programming**
- Winter wine release parties (celebrate new vintages)
- Spanish cultural festivals (seasonal celebrations: Navidad, Three Kings, Valentine's Day Spanish-style)
- Intimate chef's dinners (4-8 seats, experimental menus, premium pricing $150-200/person)
- Corporate team-building (winter off-season for many companies; push in Q1)
- Private tastings/cooking classes

**B. Covered Cellar Door Year-Round**
- Build weather protection for cellar door seating (critical for winter operation)
- Enables consistent walk-in revenue regardless of weather
- Casual experience still available in winter

**C. International E-Commerce (Winter-Focused)**
- Australia/NZ winter = Northern Hemisphere summer (holiday gift buying peak)
- Wine club subscriptions (quarterly shipments; counter-seasonal revenue)
- Food product gift sets (olive oil, olives, preserves bundles)
- Leverage winter holidays for e-commerce surge (Christmas, New Year, Valentine's Day)

**D. Corporate Entertainment**
- Auckland companies planning off-site events; winter is planning season
- Exclusive wine tastings, private cooking experiences
- Merchandise gifts (Casita Miro branded glasses, serviettes, cookbook)

**Projected Impact:** Convert 40-50% of summer revenue to winter through diversified programming + e-commerce

---

### Challenge #2: Selling More Kitchen Products (Shop + Online)
**Problem:** On-premise products only sold to local vendors; no real retail strategy

**Solution Strategy:**

**A. Professional Product Range & Branding**
- Standardize and scale current products: olive oil, olives, honey, walnuts, pesto
- Premium positioning (not mass-market; think artisanal, hand-crafted, authentic Spanish)
- Movida-style curation: "We source the finest Spanish goods + create our own"

**B. On-Site Shop Experience**
- Redesign shop as destination (not afterthought)
- Clear product storytelling: where it's sourced (Spain vs. Waiheke), why it matters, how to use
- High-margin impulse purchases: small bottles of olive oil, conservas, Spanish imports
- Tasting opportunity: open olives, oils, honey for customers to sample before buying

**C. Online Direct-to-Consumer**
- E-commerce site featuring:
  - Casita Miro proprietary products (oils, olives, honey, walnuts, pesto)
  - Curated Spanish imports (jamón ibérico, cheeses, conservas, wines, sherries)
  - Gift sets (seasonal bundles, holiday presents)
  - Subscription boxes (quarterly "taste of Spain" box with new products + recipes)
  - Merchandise (Movida-style ceramics collaboration; branded glassware, serviettes, aprons)

**D. Retail Partnerships (National)**
- Specialty food shops in Auckland (Moore Wilson, Nadene's, niche grocers)
- Gourmet/deli chains nationally (Bin Inn, Nosh, similar)
- Online food platforms (NZ-based food retailers)
- Position as: "Authentic Spanish goods + Waiheke artisan products"

**E. Restaurant Upsell**
- Display products on dining tables (small bottles of oils, honey, walnuts to purchase)
- Menu callouts: "Try our Miro Grove olive oil at home" with product links
- Gift vouchers + product bundles for special occasions

**Projected Impact:** Kitchen products = 15-20% of revenue (currently minimal)

---

### Challenge #3: Selling More Wine (Shop + Online)
**Problem:** Wine retail underdeveloped; missing direct-to-consumer and international opportunities

**Solution Strategy:**

**A. Wine Shop Experience (On-Site)**
- Curated selection (not overwhelming; 40-60 wines max)
  - Miro Vineyard wines (core focus, estate pride)
  - Spanish regional wines (by region: Rioja, Andalusia, Catalonia, Basque Country, etc.)
  - Premium sherries (imported directly from Spanish bodegas)
- Clear storytelling: wine origins, food pairings, sommelier notes
- Staff training: every server can explain every wine
- Tasting opportunity: samples available in shop

**B. Wine Club (Recurring Revenue)**
- 3 tiers:
  - **Explorer** ($75/quarter): 3 Spanish wines + tasting notes + recipe pairing
  - **Aficionado** ($150/quarter): 4 premium wines + sommelier letter + exclusive discounts
  - **Cellar** ($250/quarter): 5 premium + exclusive releases + quarterly tasting event invite
- Deliver quarterly; ship internationally
- Build email/content around wine releases, terroir stories, Spanish wine education

**C. Online Wine Retail**
- E-commerce section with full wine list:
  - Miro Vineyard wines (estate focus)
  - Spanish wines (organized by region + style)
  - Sherries and fortified (unique positioning)
  - Madame Rouge (proprietary aperitif)
- Shipping to Australia, NZ (compliance required; handle properly)
- International shipping via existing e-cellardoor partnership (expand visibility)

**D. Sommelier-Led Content Marketing**
- Blog posts: wine pairing guides, Spanish wine regions, food + wine education
- Email content: tasting notes, new releases, seasonal recommendations
- Social content: wine pours, vineyard stories, behind-the-scenes winemaking

**E. International Market (High-Value)**
- Wine club shipping to Australia (winter = high gift-giving season; premium pricing)
- Corporate gifts: companies purchasing wine for clients (premium positioning)
- Export relationships: approach NZ wine distributors for national + international placement

**F. Experiential Wine**
- Cellar door tastings (structured, tiered pricing)
- Private tastings (by appointment; $100-150/person; curated Spanish focus)
- Wine dinner pairings (monthly or seasonal; premium pricing)

**Projected Impact:** Wine revenue = 25-30% of total (currently ~15%)
