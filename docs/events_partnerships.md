# Events & Partnerships

**Programming, experiential events, and strategic partnerships to solve seasonal challenges and build community**

---

## Events Strategy

### Goal
Create year-round programming that drives winter revenue, builds brand loyalty, and differentiates Casita Miro as an experience destination — not just a restaurant.

### Core Event Types

#### 1. Wine Releases (Quarterly)
**What:** Celebrate new Miro Vineyard vintages with exclusive tasting events  
**Timing:** March, June, September, December (4x/year)  
**Duration:** 3 hours (6pm-9pm)  
**Capacity:** 30-40 people  
**Price:** $60-80/person (includes tastings + tapas)

**Experience:**
- Barnett introduces the vintage (story, terroir, winemaking process)
- 5-6 wines tasted (including new release + food pairings)
- Opportunity to pre-order wine at member pricing
- Create community among wine enthusiasts

**Marketing:** Email announcement (4 weeks prior) + social media + word-of-mouth

---

#### 2. Spanish Cultural Celebrations (Seasonal)
**Events throughout the year:**

- **Navidad (December):** Traditional Spanish Christmas; mulled wine, Spanish pastries, gift exchange, festive atmosphere
- **Three Kings (January):** Roscón de reyes (cake) + Spanish traditions + wine
- **Valentine's Day Spanish Style (February):** "Love in Spain" — romantic tasting menu, Spanish wine pairings, storytelling
- **Harvest Festival (March):** Celebrate vineyard harvest; thank you event for wine club members

**Format:** Evening events (6-9pm) or lunchtime celebrations  
**Price:** $75-100/person or included for wine club members  
**Capacity:** 40-60 people

---

#### 3. Chef's Dinners (Monthly or Seasonal)
**What:** Intimate 8-12 person dinners with custom menus and Barnett/Cat interaction  
**Timing:** Monthly (or seasonal for off-peak)  
**Duration:** 2.5-3 hours  
**Price:** $150-200/person (premium experience)

**Experience:**
- Limited seats (intimate, exclusive feel)
- Custom menu designed around season/theme (Spanish collaborations, wine region focus, ingredient showcase)
- Wine pairings curated by Barnett
- Possibility for guest chef collaborations (other Spanish chefs or Auckland food leaders)
- Personal interaction with owners

**Themes Examples:**
- "Sherries of Southern Spain" (educate on styles, food pairings)
- "Waiheke Meets Andalusia" (blend local produce with Spanish traditions)
- "Blind Tasting Challenge" (guest participation, fun)
- "Olive Oil Masterclass" (sourcing, tasting, uses)

---

#### 4. Corporate Team-Building Events
**What:** Custom experiences for Auckland companies  
**Timing:** Weekday lunches or evening events (Q1/Q4 most popular)  
**Duration:** 2-4 hours  
**Capacity:** 20-50 people  
**Price:** $80-150/person (depending on menu + experience)

**Offerings:**
- Vineyard tour + tasting + lunch
- Wine tasting challenge (team competition)
- Cooking class with Spanish focus (interactive, team-building)
- Private dining + wine pairing (formal dinner option)
- Spanish culture workshop (language, history, traditions)

**Marketing:** LinkedIn outreach to Auckland event managers + HR departments

---

#### 5. Educational Workshops (Monthly or Seasonal)
**What:** Deep-dive learning experiences on wine, food, Spanish culture  
**Timing:** Weekend mornings or weekday evenings  
**Duration:** 90 minutes  
**Capacity:** 20-30 people  
**Price:** $45-75/person

**Topics:**
- Wine 101 (regions, varietals, tasting technique)
- Spanish Cuisine Fundamentals (techniques, ingredients, philosophy)
- Olive Oil Tasting Masterclass (sourcing, quality indicators, uses)
- Sherry Education (history, styles, food pairings)
- Waiheke Wine Country Guide (tour other wineries + local context)

**Format:** Hands-on; includes tasting; attendees take home reference materials

## Strategic Partnerships

### Tourism & Hospitality Partnerships

#### 1. Waiheke Tourism Board & Visit Waiheke
- **Goal:** Get Casita Miro featured in tourism guides, recommended itineraries
- **Tactics:**
  - Submit for wine country guides and tourism maps
  - Offer press trip (vineyard tour + lunch) to tourism writers
  - Co-promote seasonal events (Waiheke wine festival calendar)
  - Contribute to Waiheke tourism content

#### 2. Ferry Operators (Fullers, SeaSpan)
- **Goal:** Package deals (ferry + Casita Miro experience)
- **Partnership Ideas:**
  - "Wine Country Escape" bundle (ferry + vineyard tour + lunch + tasting; $150-200/person)
  - Promote on ferry booking websites and in-transit marketing
  - Discounts for frequent ferry riders (loyalty program cross-promotion)
  - Co-marketing (ferry advertises Casita Miro; we promote ferry packages)

#### 2. Auckland Hotels & Accommodation
- **Goal:** Get recommended in concierge services; capture tourists
- **Partner Hotels:**
  - Luxury hotels (Hilton, Hilton Hyatt) in Auckland
  - Waiheke accommodation (The Landing, Delamore Lodge, boutique properties)
- **Tactics:**
  - Personal outreach to concierge managers (samples, relationship building)
  - Offer concierge commission or group discounts (if they book multiple reservations)
  - Provide Casita Miro brochures in hotel rooms
  - Create concierge-exclusive packages (wine + accommodation bundles)

#### 4. Other Waiheke Wineries (Non-Competing)
- **Goal:** Cross-promotion, shared wine tours, multi-winery experiences
- **Partnership Ideas:**
  - "Waiheke Wine Trail" — create 3-4 winery itinerary with other boutique wineries
  - Guest sommelier event (invite other winery's wine expert to lead tasting at Casita Miro)
  - Bundle tasting room visits (discount or experience at each location)
  - Shared marketing for wine tourism campaigns

## Partnership Management Framework

### Outreach Process
1. **Identify partner** (matches values, audience, complementary services)
2. **Research** (understand their business, audience, needs)
3. **Personalized pitch** (explain mutual benefit, specific collaboration ideas)
4. **Meeting/call** (build relationship, negotiate terms)
5. **MOA/agreement** (if needed; outline expectations, benefits, duration)
6. **Execute** (launch promotion, share marketing assets)
7. **Monitor** (track results, engagement, customer feedback)
8. **Iterate** (adjust approach if needed; plan next phase)

### Key Partnership Elements
- **Mutual benefit** — Both parties gain something valuable
- **Clear terms** — Expectations, timelines, deliverables defined
- **Marketing support** — Cross-promotion, shared assets, visibility
- **Regular communication** — Monthly check-ins, results sharing
- **Long-term mindset** — Build sustainable relationships, not one-off deals
