## Part 1: Four Reference Websites

### Reference 1: Mudbrick Vineyard & Restaurant
**URL:** https://mudbrick.co.nz/  
**Why relevant:**
- Waiheke Island competitor with premium vineyard + restaurant model
- Strong experience-focused positioning (romantic destination)
- Clear content architecture: Eat, Stay, Wine, Tasting, Weddings, About, Shop
- Multi-restaurant model (Flagship + Archive Bistro)
- Accommodation integration (extends experience)

**Design observations:**
- Simple, clean navigation
- High-quality hero imagery (ocean views, romantic moments)
- Experience-first messaging ("Escape to the most romantic place on Earth")
- Multiple dining options (premium + casual tier pricing)

**What to learn:**
- Clear hierarchy of experiences (Eat, Stay, Wine, Tasting)
- Weddings as major marketing angle
- Shop integration (wine sales as revenue stream)
- Storytelling about founders (Nick & Robyn Jones journey)

**NOT copying:** Layout, specific design, color scheme

## Part 3: Design Direction

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Spanish Red #D32F2F (bold, confident; Movida-inspired)
- **Accent:** Wine Dark #741D1D (deeper red; calls to action)
- **Neutral:** Cream #F5F1E8 (off-white; background, warmth)
- **Text:** Dark Gray #2C2C2C (readable, sophisticated)
- **Gold accent:** #C9A961 (optional; luxury touch, award badges)

**Why this palette:**
- Moves away from generic Mediterranean blues/whites
- Spanish red is bold and immediately recognizable
- Cream provides warmth without coldness
- Dark text ensures readability and professionalism

---

### Typography
- **Headlines:** Bold serif (Georgia, Playfair Display, or similar; elegant, classic)
- **Body text:** Clean sans-serif (Inter, Mulish, or similar; readable, modern)
- **Accent:** Handwriting font (optional; signature quotes from Barnett/Cat; very minimal use)

**Why:**
- Serif headlines = premium, heritage feel (Spanish tradition)
- Sans-serif body = modern, easy to read (Movida approach without copying)
- Minimal ornamental fonts = sophisticated, not overly decorative

---

### Visual Aesthetic
- **Hero imagery:** Large, stunning photographs (vineyard, ocean views, plated dishes, family moments)
- **No animations:** Static, clean design (unlike Movida; focus on content)
- **Generous whitespace:** Breathing room; not cramped or busy
- **Photography-first:** Let images tell the story (not graphics or illustrations)
- **Responsive:** Mobile-first design; works beautifully on all devices

---

### Key Content Principles
1. **Authenticity over marketing-speak:** Real stories, not corporate jargon
2. **Transparency:** Show prices, hours, policies clearly
3. **Visual hierarchy:** Most important info (booking, wine, story) prominent
4. **User-focused:** Clear CTAs; reduce friction (booking, ordering, inquiry)
5. **Spanish-first:** Spanish heritage language, imagery, positioning throughout

---

## Part 4: Design Differentiation from Movida

**What we'll do differently:**
- ✅ Own design direction (not copying layout/structure)
- ✅ NZ/Waiheke focus (not multi-city empire)
- ✅ Single-location authenticity (vs. Movida's multi-venue expansion)
- ✅ No animations (clean, static; Movida uses movement/transitions)
- ✅ Stronger wine club integration (Movida doesn't emphasize this)
- ✅ Events-forward (wine releases, cultural celebrations, chef's dinners)
- ✅ Product retail focus (Movida's Alimentaria is separate; we integrate)
- ✅ Seasonal storytelling (Waiheke farm-to-table + Spanish imports)

**What we'll learn from Movida:**
- Bold color palette (Spanish red instead of subdued)
- Heritage storytelling (25+ year journey, founders' story)
- Wine curation as brand centerpiece
- Multiple experience tiers (premium + accessible)
- Import/sourcing expertise as differentiator
- Community engagement (newsletter, events, gatherings)
